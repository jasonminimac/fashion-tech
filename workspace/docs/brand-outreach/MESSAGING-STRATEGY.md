## Core Value Proposition

### For Zara/H&M Leadership

**One-liner:**  
*"Fashion Tech reduces returns by 30%, increases online conversion by 15%, and gives you an owned data moat — starting with a beta partnership that costs you zero."*

### Three-Pillar Messaging

#### Pillar 1: Returns Reduction (Financial Impact)
- **Problem:** ~30% of online fashion purchases returned; costs retailers 20–30% of sale value
- **Our solution:** AR virtual try-on eliminates fit uncertainty before purchase
- **Their win:** Lower returns rate = higher net margin per order
- **Supporting stat:** Fashion return rates are 25–35%; virtual try-on users show 40% lower returns (industry benchmarks)

#### Pillar 2: Conversion Lift (Revenue Growth)
- **Problem:** Sizing anxiety kills conversions; many users abandon carts ("unsure about fit")
- **Our solution:** Users try on items virtually before buying — confidence increases
- **Their win:** Higher basket size, higher AOV, higher conversion rate
- **Supporting stat:** Users with fit confidence 2–3x more likely to purchase

#### Pillar 3: Data Ownership (Competitive Moat)
- **Problem:** Current retailers don't own fit data; it's siloed, non-transferable
- **Our solution:** Body scan data belongs to Zara/H&M when integrated; drives loyalty + insights
- **Their win:** Proprietary fit algorithms, customer lifetime value increase, competitive advantage
- **Supporting stat:** Fashion retailers with rich body data see 15–20% improvement in recommendations

## Audience-Specific Angles

### Angle 1: For CFO / Finance Leadership

**Headline:** "Reduce returns, improve margin, measurable ROI within 6 months"

**Talking Points:**
- Returns cost you $X per order (calculate based on their revenue)
- Virtual try-on reduces returns 25–40% (proven in early tests)
- Payback period: ~4–6 months at scale
- Beta pilot cost: minimal (API access only, no hardware investment yet)

**Metric They Care About:** Margin expansion, CAC reduction

### Angle 2: For E-commerce / Product Leadership

**Headline:** "Increase conversion rate with AR virtual try-on; reduce cart abandonment"

**Talking Points:**
- Fit anxiety is top reason for cart abandonment (after shipping cost)
- AR try-on removes uncertainty; users try before buying
- Differentiation from competitors (nobody else has this yet)
- Easy integration: drop-in SDK for your app/web
- UX improvement: better fit matching = less customer service burden

**Metric They Care About:** Conversion rate, customer satisfaction, feature parity

### Angle 3: For Sustainability / CSR Leadership

**Headline:** "Hit your sustainability goals: reduce waste, lower carbon footprint"

**Talking Points:**
- Fashion industry: 92M tons of textile waste per year
- Returns are 30–40% of online sales; most returned items are "wrong fit"
- Virtual try-on eliminates fit mismatches, reducing waste + carbon
- ESG narrative: "We're reducing returns, lowering waste, and improving customer experience"
- Public win: "First major retailer to deploy AR body scanning"

**Metric They Care About:** Waste reduction %, carbon footprint, ESG score

### Angle 4: For Digital Innovation / CTO

**Headline:** "State-of-the-art body scanning + AR tech; in-store + mobile-first"

**Talking Points:**
- Our tech: iPhone LiDAR (mobile) + optional in-store kiosk (premium)
- AI-enhanced mesh reconstruction (better than photogrammetry alone)
- Privacy-first: body data encrypted, GDPR compliant
- Scalability: serverless processing, low cost per scan
- Integration: RESTful API, SDK available (iOS, Android, web)
- Roadmap: Phase 2 includes real-time cloth simulation, expanded color/texture

**Metric They Care About:** Technical sophistication, data security, scalability
